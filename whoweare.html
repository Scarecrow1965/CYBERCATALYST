About Us



Mission

To secure career placement for Veterans in meeting Canada’s cyber defence mission

Vision

That Veterans are personally recognized with meaningful and rewarding career opportunities advancing Canada’s cyber defence posture.

Purpose

To advocate for and provide career development support to cyber‐prepared veterans through the management of specialized micro‐ credentialing, internships, individualized recruitment services and consulting assignments.

Board Members





Patrick (Pat) Shaw

Co‐founder of Coding For Veterans and Cyber Skills for Canadians, and Founder of Cyber Catalyst Talent Solutions. Pat is a passionate catalyst of knowledge mobilization. Pat has initiated Cyber Catalyst Talent Solutions to assist veterans achieve rewarding and fulfilling careers using the skills developed through the Coding For Veterans (CFV) curriculum. Pat curated the CFV secure software development and the cyber security curriculum and learning approach aligning widely recognized industry certification exams to meet the cyber talent needs of employers. Within 3 years CFV has enrolled over 300 candidates in the curriculum and delivered online learning of over 120,000 hours in a unique self‐paced, instructor and mentor supported learning format. Cyber Catalyst is the pivotal next step for those learners to translate their efforts into meaningful and rewarding careers.

 
 

Nish Bhalla

Nish Bhalla is an Entrepreneur, Angel Investor & Advisor. He exited his Toronto based Security Compass Group, a bootstrapped Cyber Security business of 15+ years. Mr. Bhalla is a published author, known speaker who has appeared on TV & has spoken at many conferences. Nish is currently an active member of the board and helps technology startups build their business and scale them.

Roy Hart

Roy joined AWS in early 2022 as Executive Advisor, AWS Professional Services. He will support Cyber Catalyst with guidance on governance, go‐to‐market strategy and building closer ties with public and private organizations throughout North America. Roy’s experience spans 30 years across multiple sectors, including positions for the Government of Canada and the Government of Ontario. Prior to joining AWS, Roy was Chief Information Officer of the British Columbia Institute of Technology (BCIT), a public polytechnic institute located in Burnaby, British Columbia. BCIT currently serves almost 50,000 students each year across six campuses in the Metro Vancouver area. Prior to joining BCIT, Mr. Hart held a number of Chief Information Officer and IT Executive roles in Alberta and Ontario. Throughout his career, Mr. Hart has delivered on numerous large scale digital transformation and IT service modernization initiatives. Roy is actively engaged with the community through speaking engagements, individual mentorship commitments, and has chaired various successful charity campaigns. Additionally, Mr. Hart has served on a number of boards and currently serves as an advisor to a number of organizations, including Coding for Veterans.

 
 

Charles MacCorquodale

Charles has been working in enterprise IT services for 20 years. The most recent 5 years have been focussed on operating in the offensive security space ‐ as a penetration tester and senior red team operator ‐ helping develop new consulting practices and testing methodologies to continuously improve cyber defence posture in organizations with a range of needs. When not on the job, Charles is an active member of DEFCON416, TASK and many online channels offering general guidance, resume review, or hands on keyboard experience competing in CTF challenges with future hackers and cyber security professionals.

Roger Payne, CD

Following 21 years of military service, Roger built upon his Naval Communicator skills through extensive cyber training, micro‐credentialling and practicum sessions including Blue Team Operations, Vulnerability Assessment Capstone, International Capture the Flag Challenges, and Industrial Networking for Cyber Security Professionals including BCIT badging in ICS Networks and Protocols, Supervisory Systems and ICA Governance. Roger is now a civilian working for the Royal Canadian Navy as a Cyber Defence Analyst for the Atlantic Fleet Cyber Team.

 
 

Serge Blais, M.Sc, MBA

Executive Director of the University of Ottawa Professional Development Institute since 2007, providing leadership to a dedicated team and subject matter experts to create, curate and deliver professional development opportunities to leaders and decision makers in government, private and public sector organizations, through partnerships that include short courses, applied certificates, and special events.

Sherry Rumbolt

Sherry is Senior Cybersecurity Strategic Advisor, Horizontal Strategic Operations at Shared Services Canada where she is instrumental in recruiting talent to support the Government of Canada’s cyber security posture. Prior to this she held the role of Senior Information Security Officer at Western Region of the Department of National Defence leading the Security Department for the Regional Service Management Center, providing Security Advisory Services, Security Incident Management, Technical Security Oversight, Education and Awareness and support to more than 113 military units. Sherry served in the Canadian Armed Forces in Information Management and Information Technology as well as Cybersecurity for 22 years. Sherry held several Security positions within the Government of British Columbia. Sherry is passionate about educating future generations of Cybersecurity experts and spreading the word about the importance of Cybersecurity. She holds several certifications in Security including Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP) and Certified Information Security Manager (CISM). Sherry is recognized as a member of the Top 25 Women in Defence 2021 and holds the honour of being in the Top 20 Women in Cybersecurity Canada. She also serves as President of ISACA Victoria Chapter.
